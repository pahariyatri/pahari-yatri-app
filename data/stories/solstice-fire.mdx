  patience at the fire’s edge; the heart remembered a quieter alphabet.

At the ridge the wind had opinions; we thanked it and built a bright rebuttal in the fire pit. The flame did not win or lose. It negotiated. Our hands returned to us. Our faces remembered summer.

In the dark, stories arrived like patient animals. We kept them close and did not ask for tricks. When the stars made their careful grammar overhead, we read what we could and let the rest be wonder. That was more than enough.
